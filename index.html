There are thousands of satellites currently in orbit, with many satellites passing in the sky overhead at any given moment. You can receive some of them yourself with a cheap SDRSoftware Defined Radio and antenna, and with weather satellites, you can receive beautiful images of the Earth as seen from space taken by satellites in orbit, decoded in near real time.

Easily Receivable Satellites

The simplest weather satellites to receive are:

  • NOAA National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration POES Polar Orbiting Environmental Satellites satellites: NOAA 15, NOAA 18, and NOAA 19
  • Russian Meteor Satellites: Meteor M2 (Meteor M2-2's LRPT was disabled because it was hit by a micrometeorite)

The simplest SSTVSlow Scan TeleVision satellite to receive is:

  • ISSInternational Space Station Zarya Module

The simplest amateur satellite to receive is:

  • SAUDISAT 1C (SO-50)

To see sample images of what you can receive from these satellites, check out the Satellites page!

Satellite Broadcasts

You can easily receive these satellites yourself with a cheap SDRSoftware Defined Radio and antenna. More info on these satellites and their broadcasts can be found below.

NOAA POES Satellites

These satellites pass overhead multiple times a day, but you will get more passes the closer you get to the Earth's poles. These are the easiest satellites to receive as you can get something in your result even if the signal is really weak. The currently active satellites are NOAA 15, NOAA 18, and NOAA 19. Each of their signals are analogue, so if you listen to the demodulated signal, you can hear the distinct "ticks" and "beeps" of the satellite easily.
